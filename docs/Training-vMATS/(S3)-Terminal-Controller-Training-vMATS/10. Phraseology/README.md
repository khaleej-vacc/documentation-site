# 10. Phraseology

## 10.1 General

Standard phraseology ensures clear, concise, and unambiguous communication between controllers and pilots.

The Approach Controller shall use ICAO standard phraseology at all times.

---

## 10.2 General Principles

The Approach Controller shall:

- Use clear and concise instructions  
- Avoid unnecessary or non-standard words  
- Speak at a controlled and intelligible pace  
- Ensure instructions are unambiguous  

---

## 10.3 Readback Requirements

The Approach Controller shall ensure that pilots correctly read back:

- Headings  
- Altitudes and levels  
- Speeds  
- Clearances (including approach and take-off)  

Incorrect or incomplete readbacks shall be corrected immediately.

---

## 10.4 Radar Instructions

The Approach Controller shall issue clear and standard instructions:

    Turn left heading 180.  
    Climb to 5000 feet.  
    Descend to 3000 feet.  

Instructions shall include all required elements and be easily understood.

---

## 10.5 Speed Control

The Approach Controller shall use standard phraseology:

    Reduce speed to 180 knots.  
    Maintain 160 knots to 4 DME.  

Speed instructions shall be issued in sufficient time for pilot compliance.

---

## 10.6 Approach Clearance

The Approach Controller shall issue approach clearance using standard phraseology:

    Cleared ILS approach runway XX.  
    Cleared RNAV approach runway XX.  

Approach clearance shall only be issued when positioning and separation are assured.

---

## 10.7 Transfer of Communication

The Approach Controller shall transfer aircraft using standard phraseology:

    Contact Tower 118.1.  

Frequency instructions shall be clear and accurate.

---

## 10.8 Go-Around / Missed Approach

The Approach Controller or Tower Controller shall issue:

    Go around, fly runway heading, climb to 3000 feet.  

Instructions shall be immediate and unambiguous.

---

## 10.9 Limitations

The Approach Controller shall not:

- Use non-standard or informal language  
- Issue ambiguous or incomplete instructions  
- Overload the pilot with excessive information  
- Omit critical elements of an instruction  

---

## 10.10 Key Principle

Phraseology shall be standard, clear, and concise.

The Approach Controller shall:

- Use ICAO standard phraseology  
- Confirm critical instructions  
- Avoid ambiguity  

Effective phraseology reduces errors and enhances safety.
