# 9.2 APP ↔ TWR Coordination

## 9.2.1 General

Coordination between Approach (APP) and Tower (TWR) ensures the safe and efficient transfer of arriving aircraft for landing.

The Approach Controller shall deliver aircraft in a stable, correctly spaced, and predictable condition.

---

## 9.2.2 Arrival Handover

The Approach Controller shall transfer aircraft to Tower only when:

- The aircraft is established on final approach  
- The aircraft is correctly sequenced  
- The aircraft is stable (heading, speed, and descent)  
- No further vectoring or speed control is required  

Transfer shall support continuous and safe runway operations.

---

## 9.2.3 Approach Controller Responsibilities

The Approach Controller shall:

- Ensure correct spacing between arriving aircraft  
- Deliver aircraft on a stable approach profile  
- Complete all required vectoring and speed control prior to transfer  
- Coordinate with Tower where required  

The Approach Controller remains responsible until transfer of control is complete.

---

## 9.2.4 Tower Controller Responsibilities

The Tower Controller shall:

- Monitor aircraft on final approach  
- Issue landing clearance when appropriate  
- Maintain runway separation  
- Manage runway occupancy  

Tower shall not be required to correct unstable or poorly sequenced traffic.

---

## 9.2.5 Coordination Requirements

The Approach Controller shall coordinate with Tower when:

- Spacing is non-standard  
- Traffic demand is high  
- Multiple arrivals are closely sequenced  
- Abnormal or priority situations exist  

Coordination shall be explicit and timely.

---

## 9.2.6 Transfer of Responsibility

Transfer of communication occurs when the aircraft is instructed to contact Tower.

Transfer of control occurs when:

- Tower accepts the aircraft, or  
- Two-way communication is established, as defined by local procedures  

The Approach Controller remains responsible until transfer of control is complete.

---

## 9.2.7 Limitations

The Approach Controller shall not:

- Transfer aircraft that are unstable  
- Transfer aircraft with insufficient spacing  
- Transfer aircraft requiring further control intervention  

---

## 9.2.8 Key Principle

Approach shall deliver aircraft in a condition requiring no corrective action from Tower.

The Approach Controller shall:

- Plan ahead  
- Sequence correctly  
- Ensure stable approaches  

A correct handover enables safe and efficient runway operations.
