# 03.3 Separation Responsibility

## Training Objective

To define ownership and application of separation.

---

## Key Knowledge

- When ATC is responsible for separation
- Transfer of responsibility
- Pilot vs controller responsibility

---

## Operational Application

Controllers shall:

- Maintain separation between controlled aircraft
- Ensure separation is transferred correctly
- Monitor until responsibility is transferred

---

## 1. Overview

Separation responsibility lies with the controller once radar control is established.

---

## 2. Core Content

### 2.1 Controller Responsibility

- Applies to all identified aircraft under control
- Continues until transfer of control

### 2.2 Transfer of Responsibility

- Occurs at defined coordination points
- Must be clearly established

### 2.3 Pilot Responsibility

- Applies in specific cases (e.g. visual separation if approved)

---

## 3. Procedures

### 3.1 Handover

- Separation shall exist at the point of transfer
- Receiving controller assumes responsibility upon acceptance

---

## 4. Standards

- Controllers shall not transfer conflicted traffic
- Separation must be assured prior to transfer

---

## 5. Notes

- Assumptions during handover are a major risk factor
