# 03.2 Priorities of Control

## Training Objective

To establish the correct priority order for controller actions.

---

## Key Knowledge

- Safety vs efficiency
- Task prioritisation
- Workload management

---

## Operational Application

Controllers shall prioritise:

1. Separation
2. Collision avoidance
3. Traffic flow
4. Efficiency

---

## 1. Overview

Controllers must continuously prioritise tasks to ensure safety is never compromised.

---

## 2. Core Content

### 2.1 Primary Priority: Separation

- Maintaining separation is the highest priority
- All other tasks are secondary

### 2.2 Secondary Priority: Collision Avoidance

- Immediate action required if separation is at risk

### 2.3 Traffic Flow

- Sequencing and spacing
- Efficient routing

### 2.4 Efficiency

- Direct routings
- Optimal levels

---

## 3. Procedures

### 3.1 Task Management

- Controllers shall reassess priorities continuously
- Lower priority tasks may be delayed if required

---

## 4. Standards

- Separation shall never be compromised for efficiency

---

## 5. Notes

- High workload requires strict prioritisation discipline
