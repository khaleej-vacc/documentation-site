# 04.1 Traffic Flow Principles

## Training Objective

To provide an understanding of how traffic flows through an ACC sector and how controllers influence that flow.

---

## Key Knowledge

- Traffic flow vs individual control
- Predictability and stability
- Strategic vs tactical control

---

## Operational Application

Controllers shall:

- Maintain a stable and predictable traffic flow
- Anticipate traffic patterns and conflicts
- Apply control measures to prevent disruption

---

## 1. Overview

Traffic flow refers to the movement of multiple aircraft through a sector in a structured and manageable manner.

---

## 2. Core Content

### 2.1 Flow Stability

- Stable flows reduce workload
- Unstable flows increase complexity

### 2.2 Predictability

- Predictable aircraft behaviour improves planning
- Sudden changes increase risk

### 2.3 Strategic vs Tactical Control

- Strategic: planning ahead (10–20 minutes)
- Tactical: immediate actions

---

## 3. Procedures

### 3.1 Flow Management

- Controllers shall manage flows, not just individual aircraft
- Early action shall be taken to prevent congestion

---

## 4. Standards

- Traffic flow shall remain manageable at all times

---

## 5. Notes

- Reactive control leads to instability
