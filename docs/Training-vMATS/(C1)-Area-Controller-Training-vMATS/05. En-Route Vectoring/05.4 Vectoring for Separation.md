# 05.4 Vectoring for Separation

## Training Objective

To apply vectoring techniques to maintain or restore separation.

---

## Key Knowledge

- Lateral separation through headings
- Conflict geometry
- Timing of intervention

---

## Operational Application

Controllers shall:

- Use vectoring to resolve conflicts early
- Apply sufficient heading change to ensure separation
- Monitor until conflict is resolved

---

## 1. Overview

Vectoring is a primary method for resolving conflicts in radar environments.

---

## 2. Core Content

### 2.1 Conflict Resolution

- Heading changes alter aircraft track
- Separation is achieved through divergence

### 2.2 Timing

- Early action reduces required deviation
- Late action increases workload and risk

### 2.3 Magnitude of Turn

- Must be sufficient to ensure separation
- Minor corrections may be ineffective

---

## 3. Procedures

### 3.1 Application

- Identify conflict
- Issue heading instruction
- Monitor until separation assured

---

## 4. Standards

- Separation minima shall not be infringed

---

## 5. Notes

- Insufficient turns are a common cause of loss of separation
