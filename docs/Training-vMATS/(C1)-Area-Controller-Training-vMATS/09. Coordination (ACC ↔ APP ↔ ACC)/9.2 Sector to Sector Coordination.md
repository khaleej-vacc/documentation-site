# 09.2 Sector to Sector Coordination

## Training Objective

To ensure safe and efficient transfer of aircraft between sectors within an ACC.

---

## Key Knowledge

- Transfer of control vs communication
- Coordination requirements
- Standard transfer conditions

---

## Operational Application

Controllers shall:

- Transfer aircraft in a coordinated and stable condition
- Ensure no conflicts exist at the boundary
- Comply with agreed sector procedures

---

## 1. Overview

Sector to sector coordination ensures the safe and orderly transfer of aircraft between controllers within the same ACC.

---

## 2. Core Content

### 2.1 Internal ACC Transfers

Aircraft shall be transferred between sectors:

- At agreed levels  
- On agreed routing  
- With no outstanding conflicts  

---

### 2.2 Transfer of Control

- Responsibility transfers at defined coordination point  
- Receiving controller assumes separation responsibility upon acceptance  

---

### 2.3 Transfer of Communication

- May occur before or after transfer of control  
- Shall be clearly understood between controllers  

---

## 3. Procedures

### 3.1 Transferring Controller

- Resolve conflicts prior to transfer  
- Ensure aircraft is stable (level, speed, route)  
- Coordinate any non-standard conditions  

---

### 3.2 Receiving Controller

- Accept aircraft only when able to provide separation  
- Clarify any uncertainty prior to acceptance  

---

## 4. Standards

- Aircraft shall not be transferred in conflict  
- Coordination shall be completed prior to boundary  

---

## 5. Notes

- Poor coordination is a major cause of loss of separation  
- Assumptions between controllers increase risk
